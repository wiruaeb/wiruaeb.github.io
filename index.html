Fishing in Lake Michigan

A small webpage on the importance of marine wildlife in Lake Michigan

Economic importance of fish in Lake Michigan

Fishing in West Michigan is a huge part of the culture. Not only does it feed people, but it is also a huge industry that helps the economy during the summer months.

Charter fishermen open for business, as well as many of the seasonal restaurants. Fish are able to supply money to these communities to help keep them afloat.

Lake Michigan Salmon/Trout lifecycle

- Spawn within inland stream/river

- Feed while in the river, grow to adolescence

- Travel downstream to the lake

- Feed on Alewife/other bait fish until matured for about 2-4 years

- Return to the same stream they were spawned in, spawn their own eggs, and then die

Most popular Big Lake fish

  1. King Salmon or Chinook
  2. Walleye
  3. Steelhead
  4. Whitefish
